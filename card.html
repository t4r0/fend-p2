Blue Jay

Blue Jays aren’t actually blue. In fact, the pigment in their feathers is brown. Scattering light in the structural parts of the feathers causes us to see the blue coloration.

  • Scientific Name: Cyanocitta cristata
  • Average Length: 22 – 30 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 7 years
  • Habitat: Mixed Forests

The blue jay (Cyanocitta cristata) is a passerine bird in the family Corvidae, native to North America.

It has a black, U-shaped collar around its neck and a black border behind the crest. Genders are similar in size and plumage, and plumage does not vary throughout the year. Four subspecies of the blue jay are recognized.
